Design Challenge 1

Which two websites are you most inspired by?

For this website for a land development company, I looked to Mortenson (www.mortenson.com) as well as Westbank (westbankcorp.com).

How do they exhibit effective use of space, size or balance?

Both websites incorporate a beautifully modern and balanced style through generous use of white space and a modular take on a grid. Images fit within each website's brand aesthetic nicely, and the use of text is clear and indicative of the information type it is (headline, body copy, etc).

What makes them similar and different?

Both websites have complex sitemaps and architecture, but whereas Mortenson introduces with a simple set of rotating full-screen background images and headlines, Westbank puts their architectural portfolio on full display in a cascading set of images down a long, 3D-animated home page. Both websites make use of a large dropdown master menu, but whereas Mortenson only relies on this method for one subset of information as a bridge between two complete sets of architectures (each subgroup within "Work" forms essentially a mini website within the larger one), Westcorp relies on the dropdown to excess, and thus negates some of its helpful qualities through confusing navigation.

Thumbnail sketches

I played with a number of different types of grid structures, from very basic full-width text to evenly spaced 3 or 6-column grids and even played around with a less strict grid, with overlapping images. I used a big headline for H1 and smaller headlines for H2 placement. I kept a consistent logo and main nav throughout. I also kept to similar layouts for v1 of each version and then a second set of layouts for the v2's. I did the home pages last and let go of my primary focus on content placement and played more with a visually striking layout.
